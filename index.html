Denver Highlights

🎸 Red Rocks: Even without a concert, the amphitheatre and Trading Post Trail are spectacular

💰 Budget Tip: Embassy Suites free breakfast + evening reception saves ~$100/day

DAY 4 — Mon Jun 14 Pick Up RV → RMNP → Grand Lake 🚐 ~90 mi / 3 hrs with stops ▼

Activities

  • Pick up RV at Cruise America Denver depot (9am)
  • RV orientation & walkthrough
  • Drive to Estes Park (gateway to RMNP, ~1.5 hrs)
  • Enter Rocky Mountain NP, drive the legendary Trail Ridge Road
  • Stop at Alpine Visitor Center (11,796 ft!)
  • Exit west side → camp near Grand Lake

Rocky Mountain NP

⛰️ Trail Ridge Road: Highest continuous paved road in North America — 12,183 ft!

🎫 Timed Entry: Reserve at recreation.gov

Where we stay:

🏕️ Timber Creek Campground, RMNP West (single night)
🏔️Trail Ridge Road🦌Elk Meadows⛰️Alpine Visitor Center
DAY 5 — Tue Jun 15 Grand Lake → Grand Teton NP 🚐 ~480 mi / 7 hrs (long day) ▼
🗺️ Route: Grand Lake → Grand Teton

Activities

  • Early departure — the longest drive of the trip
  • Head north on US-40 → I-80 W through Rawlins, WY
  • Lunch stop in Rock Springs or Pinedale, WY
  • Scenery shifts from plains to mountain valleys
  • Arrive at Grand Teton NP by late afternoon
  • First views of the Teton Range — jaw-dropping
  • Set up camp at Colter Bay RV Park, early night

Road Trip Tips

🎵 Download playlists/audiobooks before leaving — Wyoming has spotty service

⛽ Fill up in Rawlins and Rock Springs

🎮 Travel games, audiobooks, car bingo for the kids

Where we stay:

🏕️ Colter Bay RV Park, Grand Teton NP — full hookups
🏔️Teton Range🌅Wyoming Open Road
DAY 6 — Wed Jun 16 Grand Teton National Park 🏞️ Park day ▼

Activities

  • Sunrise at Schwabacher Landing (iconic reflection shot)
  • Boat shuttle across Jenny Lake → hike to Hidden Falls & Inspiration Point (2.4 mi)
  • Lunch on the Jenny Lake shore or at the Lodge
  • Drive the 42-mile Scenic Loop Road
  • Mormon Row historic barns
  • Evening: Oxbow Bend at sunset to spot moose

Must-See

🦌 Wildlife: Moose, elk, bald eagles, bears

📸 Photo ops: Schwabacher, Mormon Row, Snake River Overlook

Where we stay:

🏕️ Colter Bay RV Park (Night 2)
🌊Jenny Lake🏚️Mormon Row Barns🦌Oxbow Bend Moose📸Schwabacher Landing
DAY 7 — Thu Jun 17 Grand Teton → Yellowstone (Fishing Bridge) 🚐 ~80 mi / 2 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Grand Teton → Fishing Bridge

Activities

  • Easy morning at Colter Bay, pack up
  • Drive north into Yellowstone via South Entrance
  • Stop at Lewis Falls
  • Continue to Fishing Bridge RV Park — set up camp for 4 nights!
  • West Thumb Geyser Basin — geothermal pools on Yellowstone Lake
  • Dinner at Lake Hotel or at camp
  • Explore Fishing Bridge / Lake area, walk the boardwalk

Home Base for 4 Nights!

🏕️ Fishing Bridge RV Park is the only full-hookup RV park inside Yellowstone — central location, showers, laundry, dump station, RV store across the street

🧊 Stock the fridge — dining options inside the park are limited and pricey

Where we stay:

🏕️ Fishing Bridge RV Park — full hookups (Night 1 of 4)
🌋Yellowstone🌅Yellowstone Lake
DAY 8 — Fri Jun 18 Yellowstone — Old Faithful & Grand Prismatic 🏞️ Day trip ~100 mi ▼

Activities

  • Early departure — drive to Old Faithful (~1 hr from Fishing Bridge)
  • Watch Old Faithful erupt (every ~90 min)
  • Walk the Upper Geyser Basin boardwalk — Morning Glory Pool, Castle Geyser
  • Stroll through Old Faithful Inn log lobby
  • Lunch at Old Faithful
  • Grand Prismatic Spring — from the overlook trail for the rainbow view
  • Norris Geyser Basin on the way back if time allows
  • Back to Fishing Bridge for dinner

Yellowstone Fun Facts

🌋 Yellowstone sits on a supervolcano

💨 Old Faithful erupts ~17 times per day

🌈 Grand Prismatic is 370 ft wide — the largest hot spring in the US

Where we stay:

🏕️ Fishing Bridge RV Park (Night 2 of 4)
💨Old Faithful🌈Grand Prismatic♨️Morning Glory Pool🏨Old Faithful Inn
DAY 9 — Sat Jun 19 Yellowstone — Grand Canyon & Hayden Valley 🏞️ Short drives from camp ▼

Activities

  • Morning: Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone — Artist Point
  • Walk the North Rim and South Rim drives — multiple overlooks
  • Brink of Lower Falls trail (steep but kid-doable, 328 steps down)
  • Lunch at Canyon Village
  • Afternoon: Hayden Valley drive — bison herds galore
  • Bring binoculars, scan for grizzlies along Yellowstone River
  • Sunset at Hayden Valley — prime wildlife hour
  • S'mores at camp

Kid-Favorite Day

📏 Lower Falls: 308 feet — twice the height of Niagara

🐃 Hayden Valley has the densest bison concentration in the park

⚠️ Safety: Stay 25 yards from bison, 100 yards from bears/wolves

Where we stay:

🏕️ Fishing Bridge RV Park (Night 3 of 4)
💧Lower Falls🗻Artist Point🐃Hayden Valley Bison🌅Yellowstone Canyon
DAY 10 — Sun Jun 20 Yellowstone — Lamar Valley & Mammoth 🏞️ Day trip ~150 mi ▼

Activities

  • Pre-dawn departure (tough but so worth it!) — drive to Lamar Valley
  • Wolf-watching at Lamar Valley at first light — "America's Serengeti"
  • Bring binoculars; wolf-watchers often set up scopes at pullouts and share
  • Spot bison herds with calves, pronghorn, coyotes, bald eagles
  • Late breakfast at Roosevelt Lodge
  • Tower Fall stop
  • Afternoon: Mammoth Hot Springs travertine terraces
  • Drive back to Fishing Bridge via Norris for dinner (or eat in Mammoth)

Wildlife Gold Day

🐺 Lamar Valley at dawn is your best wolf-sighting chance in the Lower 48

🔭 A cheap spotting scope or good binoculars makes this day 10x better

🏔️ Mammoth terraces look like a geothermal wedding cake — unique in the world

Where we stay:

🏕️ Fishing Bridge RV Park (Night 4 of 4, final night!)
🐺Lamar Valley🐃Bison Herds♨️Mammoth Terraces🕍Travertine
DAY 11 — Mon Jun 21 Yellowstone → Cody, WY 🚐 ~80 mi / 2 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Yellowstone → Cody

Activities

  • Leisurely morning, break camp at Fishing Bridge
  • Exit Yellowstone via the East Entrance — most scenic route out!
  • Drive through Wapiti Valley (Teddy Roosevelt called it “the most scenic 50 miles in America”)
  • Arrive in Cody, WY
  • Visit the Buffalo Bill Center of the West — 5 museums in one! Plains Indians, firearms, Western art, natural history, Buffalo Bill himself
  • Kids love the Plains Indian Museum and the Draper Natural History Museum
  • Evening: Cody Nite Rodeo (runs nightly all summer — "Rodeo Capital of the World")

Cody Highlights

🎷 Buffalo Bill Center is often rated one of the best museums in the American West — budget 3-4 hours minimum

🐴 Cody Nite Rodeo: An authentic Western experience, kids under 12 often half price

Where we stay:

🏕️ Ponderosa Campground, Cody WY (single night)
🏇Cody, WY🏛️Buffalo Bill Center
DAY 12 — Tue Jun 22 Cody → Thermopolis → Lander 🚐 ~200 mi / 4 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Cody → Thermopolis → Lander

Activities

  • Morning: one more stop at Buffalo Bill Center if kids want
  • Drive south to Hot Springs State Park in Thermopolis, WY (~85 mi, 1.5 hrs)
  • Swim at the State Bath House — free! (yes, really free) Home to the world's largest mineral hot springs
  • Kids will love the hot soak + the (small) water slides at nearby commercial pools
  • See the Rainbow Terraces and the bison herd in the state park
  • Lunch in Thermopolis
  • Continue south to Lander, WY (~120 mi, 2.5 hrs)
  • Camp overnight in Lander — quaint gateway town to Wind River Range

Thermopolis Magic

🕍 The State Bath House maintains a 104°F pool open to everyone for free — it’s written into the 1896 treaty with the Shoshone and Arapaho that bought the springs

🦣 The state park even has a small wild bison herd to spot

Where we stay:

🏕️ Sinks Canyon State Park Campground, Lander WY (single night)
🕍Hot Springs State Park🌄Thermopolis
DAY 13 — Wed Jun 23 Lander → Cheyenne, WY 🚐 ~250 mi / 4.5 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Lander → Cheyenne

Activities

  • Morning walk in Lander or Sinks Canyon State Park (5 mi from town — where the Popo Agie River disappears underground!)
  • Drive southeast through the Red Desert — vast open Wyoming
  • Lunch stop in Rawlins or Medicine Bow
  • Arrive Cheyenne, Wyoming's capital, mid-afternoon
  • Visit the Cheyenne Frontier Days Old West Museum
  • Walk around downtown Cheyenne — historic Wyoming State Capitol, big boot statues, Union Pacific depot
  • Dinner in Cheyenne

The Cowboy State Tour

🤠 Cheyenne calls itself the Magic City of the Plains and hosts the world's largest outdoor rodeo (Frontier Days) every July

🌞 Sinks Canyon near Lander is a geological oddity: the river sinks into a cave and emerges from a spring downstream

Where we stay:

🏕️ Cheyenne KOA (single night)
DAY 14 — Thu Jun 24 Cheyenne → Estes Park & RMNP East 🚐 ~100 mi / 2 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Cheyenne → Estes Park

Activities

  • Relaxed morning departure — short drive today!
  • Cross back into Colorado
  • Arrive Estes Park — the east-side gateway to RMNP
  • Check in at RV resort, set up camp for 2 nights
  • Afternoon: enter RMNP east side — completely different feel from the west!
  • Bear Lake — easy 0.8 mi paved loop around the lake, stunning
  • Drive to Sprague Lake or Dream Lake for another short hike
  • Stroll Estes Park downtown — Taffy Shop (since 1935!), Ben & Jerry's

Welcome to the East Side

🐻 Elk are everywhere in Estes Park — they literally walk down main street in summer evenings

🏬 Stay the iconic Stanley Hotel (from The Shining) is a fun drive-by or lunch stop

Where we stay:

🏕️ Estes Park KOA Holiday — full hookups (Night 1 of 2)
🏘️Estes Park Town🏞️Bear Lake🏔️RMNP East Side
DAY 15 — Fri Jun 25 RMNP East Day → Denver 🚐 ~75 mi / 1.5 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Estes Park → Denver

Activities

  • Sunrise at Bear Lake — breathtaking when the light hits
  • Emerald Lake Trail (3.6 mi round trip, moderate) — best day hike on the east side
  • Or easier option: Alberta Falls (1.6 mi) + Nymph Lake

  • Afternoon: last wildlife drive along Moraine Park for elk
