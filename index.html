DAY BY DAY ITINERARY

DAY 1 — Wed Jun 11 Cleveland → Asheville 🚐 ~450 mi / 7 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Cleveland → AshevilleOpen in Google Maps ↗

Activities

  • Pick up RV at Cruise America Cleveland early morning
  • Hit the road! I-77 South through West Virginia
  • Scenic stretch through New River Gorge area
  • Arrive Asheville by late afternoon
  • Set up camp, grab dinner at a local spot
  • Early night — big day at Biltmore tomorrow!

Driving Day

🚐 Long but scenic drive through WV mountains. Plenty of rest stops along I-77 and I-79.

💡 Tip: Stop in Beckley, WV for lunch — roughly halfway

Where we stay:

🏕️ Rutledge Lake RV Resort, Asheville (Night 1 of 2)
DAY 2 — Thu Jun 12 Biltmore Estate + River Arts District 🏰 Full day in Asheville ▼

Activities

  • Biltmore Estate — America's largest home! Plan 4-5 hours
  • Tour the 250-room mansion, gardens, and winery
  • Kids love the farm area with goats, chickens, and horses
  • Afternoon: River Arts District — galleries, studios, street art
  • Dinner at 12 Bones Smokehouse — famous Asheville BBQ

Asheville Day

🏰 Biltmore tickets: Adults ~$95, Youth (10-16) ~$50, Under 10 FREE. Buy online to save $5/ticket.

🎨 River Arts District is free to explore — dozens of artist studios open to visitors

💰 Tip: Keep your Biltmore ticket — it includes a free next-day grounds pass!

Where we stay:

🏕️ Rutledge Lake RV Resort, Asheville (Night 2 of 2)
DAY 3 — Fri Jun 13 Blue Ridge Parkway → Pigeon Forge 🚐 ~115 mi / 4 hrs (scenic) ▼
🗺️ Route: Asheville → Pigeon Forge (via Blue Ridge Parkway)Open in Google Maps ↗

Activities

  • Drive the Blue Ridge Parkway — America's most scenic road!
  • Stop at overlooks: Devil's Courthouse, Richland Balsam (highest point on BRP at 6,053 ft)
  • Waterfalls along the way: Looking Glass Falls (easy roadside stop)
  • Take US-441 into Pigeon Forge area
  • Arrive at Camp Margaritaville — our home base for 5 nights!
  • Evening: Explore The Island in Pigeon Forge — Ferris wheel, dinner, shops

Scenic Drive Day

⛰️ The Blue Ridge Parkway is 25-45 mph with no commercial vehicles. Allow extra time for stops!

📷 Don't miss: Waterrock Knob (Mile 451.2) — panoramic 360° views

🏠 Camp Margaritaville: Full-hookup RV sites, pool, lazy river, restaurant. The kids will love this one.

Where we stay:

🏕️ Camp Margaritaville, Pigeon Forge (Night 1 of 5)
DAY 4 — Sat Jun 14 Cades Cove + Laurel Falls 🏞️ GSMNP Day 1 ▼

Activities

  • Cades Cove — go early (before 8 AM) to beat traffic!
  • 11-mile one-way loop: look for black bears, deer, wild turkeys
  • Stop at historic cabins and churches along the loop
  • Lunch break: picnic at the Cades Cove Picnic Area
  • Afternoon: Laurel Falls Trail — 2.6 mi round trip, paved, beautiful 80-ft waterfall
  • Easy hike, great for both kids — the reward is spectacular

Smokies Day 1

🐻 Wildlife tip: Cades Cove has the highest concentration of black bears in the park. Early morning = best chance!

🚶 Laurel Falls is the most popular trail in the park — go in the afternoon when crowds thin out

⚠️ GSMNP is free! No entrance fee — one of the few free national parks

Where we stay:

🏕️ Camp Margaritaville, Pigeon Forge (Night 2 of 5)
DAY 5 — Sun Jun 15 Dollywood — Full Day! 🎢 Theme park day ▼

Activities

  • Dollywood — arrive at park opening!
  • Top rides: Lightning Rod, Wild Eagle, Thunderhead, Mystery Mine
  • Splash Country water park if you add combo tickets
  • The kids will love Wildwood Grove area
  • Catch a live show — Dollywood's entertainment is top-notch
  • Don't miss the cinnamon bread from The Grist Mill
  • Stay until closing for shorter lines in the evening

Dollywood Day

🎫 Ticket prices: Adult (10+) ~$95, Child (4-9) ~$85. Your 12yo pays adult rate, 9yo gets child rate.

🚗 Parking: ~$25. Arrive early — the lot fills up on summer weekends.

💰 Tip: Check for AAA, employer, or credit union discounts — even $10/ticket = $40 back for the family

🌮 Eat lunch at the park or bring snacks — no re-entry after leaving

Where we stay:

🏕️ Camp Margaritaville, Pigeon Forge (Night 3 of 5)
DAY 6 — Mon Jun 16 Pigeon River Rafting! 🛶 Adventure day ▼

Activities

  • Drive to Hartford, TN (~30 min from camp via I-40)
  • Smoky Mountain River Rat — Upper Pigeon River rafting (4.9⭐, 1,362 reviews!)
  • Upper Pigeon: Class III rapids — dam-controlled, safe for ages 8+, min 70 lbs
  • Morning/midday trip, ~2.5 hours on the water
  • Dry off, grab lunch in Hartford
  • Relaxed afternoon back at camp — pool, lazy river, decompress
  • Evening: easy dinner at camp or cook in the RV

Rafting Day

🛶 River Rat: ~$50/person. Book in advance — trips fill up in peak season!

⚠️ Age/weight: Min age 8, min 70 lbs for Upper Pigeon. Your 9yo qualifies! If smaller, the Lower Pigeon (all ages 3+) is a great fallback.

💡 What to bring: Water shoes, change of clothes, dry bag for phone. They provide life jackets and helmets.

💰 Tip: Book a 4pm+ trip to save ~$10/person

Where we stay:

🏕️ Camp Margaritaville, Pigeon Forge (Night 4 of 5)
DAY 7 — Tue Jun 17 Kuwahi (Kuwahi (Clingmans Dome)) + Gatlinburg 🏞️ GSMNP Day 2 + Town ▼

Activities

  • Morning: Drive to Kuwahi (Clingmans Dome) — highest point in the Smokies (6,643 ft)
  • Steep but short hike to the observation tower (0.5 mi paved) — views of 7 states on a clear day!
  • Note: Park the RV at camp and drive to Kuwahi (Clingmans Dome) — tight mountain road
  • Afternoon: Gatlinburg SkyBridge — longest pedestrian suspension bridge in North America!
  • Ober Mountain — aerial tram + seasonal activities (alpine slide, ice skating)
  • Evening: Walk the Gatlinburg strip, dinner, grab saltwater taffy

Smokies Day 2 + Gatlinburg

⛰️ Kuwahi (Clingmans Dome): Bring layers! It's 10-15°F cooler at the summit. Can be foggy — check weather first.

🌉 SkyBridge: ~$29 adult / ~$20 child. Glass-floor panels in the middle!

🎿 Ober Mountain tram: ~$17 adult / ~$12 child

🏕️ Last Smokies night! Savor it — tomorrow we head northwest to Mammoth Cave.

Where we stay:

🏕️ Camp Margaritaville, Pigeon Forge (Night 5 of 5)
DAY 8 — Wed Jun 18 Pigeon Forge → Mammoth Cave 🚐 ~320 mi / 5 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Pigeon Forge → Cave City, KYOpen in Google Maps ↗

Activities

  • Say goodbye to the Smokies and hit I-40 West / I-65 North
  • Drive through Knoxville, Nashville outskirts, and into Kentucky
  • Lunch stop in Cookeville or Bowling Green, KY
  • Arrive at Cave Country RV by mid-afternoon
  • Set up camp, take a walk around the area
  • Easy dinner — cook at the RV or eat in Cave City
  • Rest up for cave exploration tomorrow!

Transition Day

🚐 Straightforward interstate driving through eastern TN and KY. Mostly flat and easy.

💡 Nashville bypass: Take I-840 to avoid Nashville traffic — saves 20-30 min

🧀 Fun stop: Kenny's Farmhouse Cheese in Austin, KY (just off I-65) — award-winning cheese tastings!

Where we stay:

🏕️ Cave Country RV Campground, Cave City (Night 1 of 2)
DAY 9 — Thu Jun 19 Mammoth Cave + Green River Ferry 🕳️ Cave exploration day ▼

Activities

  • Morning: Mammoth Cave NP — the world's longest known cave system (420+ miles mapped!)
  • Tour 1: Domes and Dripstones (~2 hrs) — best for families, see stunning formations
  • Tour 2: Historic Tour (~2 hrs) — the classic route through giant chambers
  • Picnic lunch above ground between tours
  • Afternoon: Green River Ferry — free car ferry across the river (fun for kids!)
  • Hike the Cedar Sink Trail (1.6 mi loop) — see where the cave meets the surface
  • Relax at camp in the evening

Mammoth Cave Day

🕳️ Tour pricing: Adults ~$25-30/tour, Children (6-12) ~$14-16/tour. Book at recreation.gov

🌡️ Cave temp: A constant 54°F year-round — bring a jacket and closed-toe shoes!

🚢 Green River Ferry: FREE! The ferry has operated since the 1800s and can carry vehicles. Great photo op.

💰 Budget note: 2 tours × 4 people ≈ $175 total

Where we stay:

🏕️ Cave Country RV Campground, Cave City (Night 2 of 2)
DAY 10 — Fri Jun 20 Mammoth Cave → Kings Island 🚐 ~200 mi / 3.5 hrs ▼
🗺️ Route: Cave City → Mason, OHOpen in Google Maps ↗

Activities

  • Pack up camp and head north on I-65 to I-71
  • Easy morning drive through Louisville and Cincinnati
  • Arrive at Camp Cedar by early afternoon
  • Cedar Fair's resort campground — right next to Kings Island!
  • Afternoon: Relax at camp, explore the resort amenities
  • Pool time, mini golf, or fishing at the pond
  • Early dinner — BIG day at Kings Island tomorrow!

Travel + Rest Day

🚐 Easy drive through the Bluegrass State. Louisville is a nice rest-stop city.

🏕️ Camp Cedar: Cedar Fair's RV resort. Pool, hot tub, fire pits, close to the park entrance. $75/night.

💡 Tip: Buy Kings Island tickets online tonight — save vs. gate price (~$45 online vs $90 at gate)

Where we stay:

🏕️ Camp Cedar, Mason OH (1 night)
DAY 11 — Sat Jun 21 Kings Island → Home! 🎢 + 🚐 ~250 mi / 4 hrs ▼

Activities

  • Kings Island — arrive at park opening for shortest lines!
  • Top coasters: Orion (giga coaster, 300 ft drop!), The Beast (longest wooden coaster), Banshee
  • Planet Snoopy area for the 9yo if needed
  • Ride until mid-afternoon, then head north on I-71
  • ~4 hour drive back to Cleveland
  • Drop off RV at Cruise America on the way home
  • Home by evening — full Sunday to unpack and rest!

Grand Finale!

🎫 Kings Island tickets: ~$60 adult online, ~$50 child. 12yo pays adult rate.

🎢 Must-ride: Orion is the standout — 5,321-foot track, 91 mph top speed

💰 Tip: Silver Season Pass ($105) beats a one-day ticket if you might visit again this summer

OUR 2 NATIONAL PARKS

Two iconic parks, each completely different — misty mountain ridges and the world's longest cave.

🏔️

Great Smoky Mountains

America's most visited national park — and it's free! Cades Cove wildlife, Laurel Falls, Kuwahi (Clingmans Dome), and the famous mist over ancient mountains.

📍 TN / NC⛰️ 6,643 ft peak📅 Days 4 & 7
📷 Photos🥾 Trails
🕳️

Mammoth Cave

420+ miles of explored passages — the longest known cave system on Earth. Ranger-led tours through cathedral-sized chambers, narrow canyons, and ancient formations.

📍 Kentucky🕳️ 420+ mi mapped📅 Day 9
