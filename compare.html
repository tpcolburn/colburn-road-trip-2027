WHAT YOU GET & WHAT YOU GIVE UP

The honest trade-offs between the two options.

🎰 Vegas One-Way

Unique to this trip
  • Walking through Delicate Arch at sunset — Utah's iconic image
  • Wading up The Narrows with water shoes in the Virgin River
  • Alien-looking hoodoos at Bryce Canyon
  • Mac King comedy magic show on the Strip
  • Mandalay Bay's 11-acre pool complex with wave pool and lazy river
  • Shark Reef Aquarium walking tunnel
  • 6 national parks checked off (great for Junior Ranger collectors)
  • Hotter, more desert-adventurous vibe
What you miss
  • No wolves, few bison — minimal wildlife variety
  • Yellowstone gets just 2 rushed days
  • 100°F+ temperatures on Delicate Arch and The Narrows
  • One-way RV rental fee (~$400-500)
  • More expensive overall

🐻 Northern Loop

Unique to this trip
  • 4 full days in Yellowstone — time to actually see it all
  • Wolf-watching at dawn in Lamar Valley (once-in-a-lifetime)
  • Bison herds with calves crossing the road
  • Buffalo Bill Center of the West in Cody (5 museums in one)
  • Cody Nite Rodeo (authentic Western experience)
  • World's largest mineral hot springs at Thermopolis (free soak!)
  • Bear Lake and Emerald Lake on RMNP's east side
  • Moose at Oxbow Bend, grizzlies in Hayden Valley
  • Cooler mountain weather, no desert heat
  • Saves ~$1,115 vs the Vegas trip
What you miss
  • No Delicate Arch, no hoodoos, no Narrows — zero red rocks
  • No Las Vegas, no magic show, no Mandalay Bay pool
  • Fewer total parks (3 vs 6)
  • Potentially less variety — 16 days of mountains & grasslands
  • No Shark Reef / Strip / High Roller for the kids
